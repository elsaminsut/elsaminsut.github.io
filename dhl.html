AVANCIS GmbH

The facade of the DHL Campus is an example of how computational design, digital fabrication and environmental analytics can come together to tackle complex architectural challenges. More than just an aesthetic statement, this project showcases the power of computational design in bridging the gap between concept and realization.

The customer reached out with a clear vision in mind: a sculptural, wavy façade powered by solar modules for their parking structure. Easier said than done.

Planning

The first step was to discretize the freeform geometry into a rationalized structure that could be translated into a real-world, manufacturable building envelope. This envelope consists of a series of bands, each featuring solar modules mounted on top in the shape of a sharp B, oriented perpendicular to the facade. These bands follow a series of guide curves and are spaced apart to ensure proper ventilation in the building floors.
